# Task: Preserve lineage through the root kinograph (fix cross-root fork)

**Goal.** Re-versioning a committed kino produced a fork: `ingest_root_kinographs`
synthesized events from committed root-kinograph entries with empty parents,
discarding lineage. Once both versions were committed and pruned, neither demoted
the other → `MultipleHeads`, and `commit` became a no-op for that root.

**Done when:** revising a committed kino across roots no longer forks — `resolve`
picks a single head and `commit` is not a no-op — because lineage is carried on the
durable root record.

## Outcome
- `RootEntry` gains an optional `parents: Vec<String>` field (backward-compatible via
  `#[facet(default)]`; absent on legacy blobs = empty), validated as hashes.
- `build_root` (commit.rs) populates it from the head event's parents, captured at
  commit time while the head is still staged with authentic parents.
- `ingest_root_kinographs` (resolve.rs) synthesizes with those parents, processes
  root pointers in sorted order, and unions parents across roots on (id,version)
  collision for deterministic, order-independent head resolution.
- Files: `crates/kinora/src/{root.rs,commit.rs,resolve.rs}`.
- Tests: cross-root 2-version (the original failing repro), same-root revision
  (boundary), 3-version chain split across three roots, parent union across roots,
  RootEntry parents roundtrip, legacy-entry default, invalid-parent-hash rejection.
  Full workspace green, zero warnings.
